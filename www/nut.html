Here are some tips to help start you off in your new nutrition journey:

  • Eat healthy fats every day
  • Drink at least 100 ounces of water each day
  • Use proper portion sizes

  • What is a macronutrient?

    a macronutrient provides your body with calories/energy. There are three types of macronutritients; proteins, carbs and fats.

    What are good fats?

    Good fats help control your hunger, while boosting your metabolism. They also lower your cholesterol helping you shed some pounds and also give you shiny and healthy nails and hair.

    what are proteins?

    Proteins are the most important food to fuel your body to build muscle. Protein is a mcaronutrient, helping you build muscle mass. Many think of protein of being just animal meat but it can also be in other sources like nuts and legumes.

    Here is a list of some proteins that bodybuilding.com came up with for people to follow and get some great ideas!

    High-protein foods Top or bottom round steak (23 grams of protein per 3-ounce serving) Lean ground beef (18 grams per 3-ounce serving) Pork chops (26 grams per 3-ounce serving) Skinless chicken breast (24 grams per 3-ounce serving) Turkey breast (24 grams per 3-ounce serving) Sockeye salmon (23 grams per 3-ounce serving) Yellowfin tuna (25 grams per 3-ounce serving)

    High-protein dairy foods include: Greek yogurt (23 grams per 8-ounce serving) Cottage cheese (14 grams per half-cup serving) Eggs (6 grams per large egg) 2 percent milk (8 grams per cup)

    Some other high-protein foods are: Some canned foods, like sardines, anchovies and tuna average around 22 grams of protein per serving Navy beans (20 grams per cup) Lentils (13 grams per quarter-cup) Peanut butter (8 grams per 2 tablespoons) Mixed nuts (6 grams per 2-ounce serving) Quinoa (8 grams per 1-cup serving) Edamame (8 grams per half-cup serving) Soba noodles (12 grams per 3-ounce serving)
